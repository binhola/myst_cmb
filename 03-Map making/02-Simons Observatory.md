# Simons Observatory

The Simons Observatory (SO) is a ground-based CMB experiment, located at the Cerro Toco site in the Atacama desert, Chile. (altitude 5200 m)

One of the main goal of SO is to measure the cosmic microwave background (CMB) polarization to constraint the inflation theory through the detection of primordial gravitational waves (or B-modes).

Instruments:
- 3 small-aperture telescopes (SATs)
- 1 large-aperture telescopes (LATs)

Design:
- 60,000 transition-edge sensor (TES) bolometers
- 6 spectral bands centered between 27 and 280 GHz
  - Low Frequency (LF): 27 GHz and 39 GHz
  - Middle Frequency (MF): 93 GHz and 145 GHz
  - Ultra High Frequency (UHF): 225 GHz and 280 GHz

SATs:
- observe 2 MF configuration and 1 UHF configuration.
- each of them employs a single optics tube with TES arrays operating at $100$ mK
- 42 cm aperture
- 35$^\circ$ field of view coupled to 36 cm diameter focal plane, accommodating 7 detectors modules
- Target multipole: $30 < \ell < 300$
- Tensor-to-scalar ratio: $\sigma(r) = 0.002$
